# Campaign Brief Protocol

Campaign briefs align marketing, brand, creative, revenue, and analytics before production begins.

## When To Load

Load for campaigns, launches, demand generation, PR, influencer work, affiliate programs, brand communications, and content sprints.

## Required Inputs

- Business objective
- ICP
- Awareness stage
- Offer or CTA
- Channel
- Budget
- Timeline
- Success metric
- Brand constraints

## Method

1. Define the business objective.
2. Define the audience and awareness stage.
3. Name the single important message.
4. Define channel and format.
5. Define offer and CTA.
6. Define creative constraints.
7. Define tracking and metric contract.
8. Define production owner and approval owner.
9. Define launch date and review date.

## Decision Gates

- No ICP: brief is not production-ready.
- No single important message: creative will fragment.
- No success metric: campaign cannot be evaluated.
- No tracking plan: paid or revenue campaign cannot launch.
- No approval owner: production will stall.

## Output Format

```markdown
# Campaign Brief - [campaign]

Objective:
ICP:
Awareness stage:
Single important message:
Offer / CTA:
Channels:
Assets:
Tracking:
Success metric:
Approval:
```

## Failure Modes

- Asset List Brief: outputs listed without strategy.
- Message Pileup: many claims compete in one creative unit.
- Channel Mismatch: format does not match user intent.
- Attribution Gap: campaign launches without measurement.
