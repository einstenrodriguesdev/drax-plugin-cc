# Structured Interviewing Protocol

Structured interviewing improves hiring quality by making every candidate face the same job-relevant evaluation.

## When To Load

Load for recruiting, interview design, scorecards, talent calibration, hiring manager alignment, and post-interview debriefs.

## Required Inputs

- Role
- Level
- Hiring need
- Must-have competencies
- Nice-to-have competencies
- Interview stages
- Decision owner

## Method

1. Define role outcomes for the first 90 days.
2. Translate outcomes into competencies.
3. Create a scorecard:
   - competency
   - signal question
   - strong evidence
   - weak evidence
   - disqualifier
4. Assign each interview stage a specific purpose.
5. Ask every candidate comparable questions.
6. Score before group discussion.
7. Decide with evidence, not charisma.

## Decision Gates

- No scorecard: interview loop is not ready.
- No level definition: calibration will drift.
- No work-sample or evidence question for critical skill: risk high.
- Hiring manager override without evidence: escalate to CHRO/founder.

## Output Format

```markdown
# Interview Scorecard - [role]

## Role Outcomes
## Competencies
| Competency | Question | Strong Signal | Weak Signal | Score |
## Decision
```

## Failure Modes

- Vibe Hiring: confidence mistaken for competence.
- Moving Target: criteria change after meeting candidates.
- Halo Effect: one strength hides missing must-have skills.
- Unstructured Debrief: loudest voice wins.
