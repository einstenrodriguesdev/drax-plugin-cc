# Capability Mapping Protocol

Capability maps show what the company must be able to do, who owns it, and where gaps block strategy.

## When To Load

Load for org design, hiring plans, people strategy, learning plans, role creation, management calibration, and scaling readiness.

## Required Inputs

- Company objective
- Current team/agents
- Required outcomes
- Existing capabilities
- Constraints
- Time horizon

## Method

1. Define the strategic objective.
2. List capabilities required to achieve it.
3. Classify each capability:
   - strategic
   - managerial
   - executional
   - support
4. Assign current owner or mark missing.
5. Score maturity:
   - 0 absent
   - 1 ad hoc
   - 2 repeatable
   - 3 measured
   - 4 scalable
6. Identify highest-risk gaps.
7. Decide whether to hire, train, outsource, automate, or defer.

## Decision Gates

- Critical capability absent: strategy is not executable.
- One person owns too many critical capabilities: key-person risk.
- Capability needed after scale but not now: defer intentionally.
- Missing manager capability: execution quality will degrade.

## Output Format

```markdown
# Capability Map - [objective]

| Capability | Type | Owner | Maturity | Gap | Action |
```

## Failure Modes

- Title Map: listing roles instead of capabilities.
- Hiring Reflex: hiring before defining the missing capability.
- Founder Bottleneck: founder silently owns every critical function.
- Scale Fantasy: advanced capability built before commercial proof.
