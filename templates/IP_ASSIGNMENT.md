## Purpose

The IP Assignment Agreement transfers to the company ownership of all intellectual property created by a contributor in the course of their engagement. Without this agreement, IP may legally belong to the individual creator by default — exposing the company to ownership disputes and blocking institutional investment, acquisition, or licensing transactions. This agreement must be executed by every C-level, IC, contractor, and advisor before they begin any work.

## 2. Definitions

**"Work Product"** means any invention, discovery, concept, design, methodology, algorithm, software (source code and object code), documentation, curriculum, data model, brand asset, marketing material, process, formula, or other work — whether or not patentable or copyrightable — that the Assignor creates, develops, conceives, or contributes to, alone or jointly, during the period of engagement with the Company and:
- Within the scope of the Assignor's role; or
- Using Company resources, systems, data, or Confidential Information; or
- Relating to the Company's business, current or reasonably anticipated products, or research

**"Pre-Existing IP"** means intellectual property created by the Assignor before the Effective Date of this agreement that is listed in Schedule A.

---

## 3. Assignment of Work Product

The Assignor hereby assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Company — effective as of the moment of creation — all right, title, and interest in and to:
- All Work Product, including all associated copyright, patent rights, trade secret rights, trademark rights, and other intellectual property rights
- All rights to file applications for patents, trademarks, or registrations in any jurisdiction
- All rights to claim priority to the Work Product in any jurisdiction
- The right to sue for past, present, and future infringement of any rights in the Work Product

This assignment is worldwide, perpetual, irrevocable, and royalty-free.

## 6. Cooperation and Further Assurances

The Assignor agrees to:
- Execute any additional documents reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, register, or enforce the assigned rights

## 8. Work Made for Hire

## 9. Representations and Warranties

The Assignor represents and warrants that:
- They have the full right and authority to make this assignment
- The Work Product, to the best of their knowledge, does not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party
- The Work Product does not include any third-party material that would restrict the Company's use of the Work Product without the Company's prior written knowledge and consent
- They are not bound by any prior agreement that conflicts with this agreement

## Interview inputs (ask one at a time)

1. In which jurisdiction is this agreement governed, and where are contributors primarily based?
2. Does the company intend this agreement to cover all employment types — employees, independent contractors, and advisors equally?
3. Should contributors be required to disclose newly created Work Product within a specific number of days?
4. What is the company's policy on contributors incorporating open-source libraries into Work Product — is prior approval required?
5. Are there any categories of third-party content (stock images, licensed fonts, open-source frameworks) that are pre-approved for incorporation?
6. Should the pre-existing IP schedule be mandatory for all contributors, or only for technical roles?
7. To what extent are moral rights waivable under the primary jurisdiction's law? Has legal counsel confirmed this?
8. How should disputes about IP ownership be resolved — litigation, arbitration, or mediation?
9. Does the company need patent assignment capability, or is copyright assignment the primary concern at this stage?
10. What approval process applies when a contributor wants to use a third-party component with a restrictive license?
