# BOARD CHARTER

> **Owner:** chairman
> **Approver:** board
> **Chain role:** Formally delimits board meeting cadence, committee agendas (Audit Committee, Technology Committee), and the separation between long-term strategy (board) and daily execution (CEO). Keeps the Chairman from intervening in C-level operations.
> **Status:** DRAFT
> **Gate:** G-governance (ratified at the first constituted board meeting)
> **Consistency:** must not contradict CHAIRMAN_LETTER.md or VISION.md.

---

## Purpose

The Board Charter establishes how the Board of Directors exercises its authority, the cadence and format of its meetings, the mandates of its standing committees, and the clear boundary between board-level strategic oversight and CEO-level operational execution. It is the primary instrument preventing unauthorized intervention by any board member — including the Chairman — into day-to-day operations.

---

## 1. Board Role and Scope of Authority

The Board is responsible for:
- Approving and monitoring the company's strategic direction
- Overseeing financial integrity and risk posture
- Approving the annual budget and any material departures from it
- Appointing, evaluating, and where necessary replacing the CEO
- Approving reserved matters as defined in SHAREHOLDERS_AGREEMENT.md

The Board is **not** responsible for:
- Day-to-day operational decisions
- Directing C-level officers other than the CEO
- Approving transactions below the threshold defined in AUTHORITY_MATRIX.md
- Intervening in hiring or dismissal of employees below C-level

## 6. Chairman Boundary Rules

The Chairman may:
- Chair board meetings and set the strategic agenda
- Represent the company at the highest ceremonial and stakeholder level when delegated by the CEO
- Engage directly with the CEO on matters of strategic direction
- Initiate a board meeting at any time

The Chairman may **not**:
- Issue instructions directly to any C-level officer other than the CEO
- Intervene in recruitment, dismissal, or compensation of employees below the CEO
- Override a CEO operational decision without triggering a board resolution
- Commit the company to any external obligation without board and CEO approval

**Breach of this boundary:** `<NEEDS_DECISION: consequence — e.g., board vote to censure>`

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

Any board member with a material interest in a matter before the board must:
1. Disclose the conflict before discussion begins
2. Abstain from the vote on that matter
3. Leave the meeting room during deliberation if requested by the Chairman
