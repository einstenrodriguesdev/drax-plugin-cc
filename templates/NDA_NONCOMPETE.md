## 2. Definitions

**"Confidential Information"** means any information, data, or material — in any form — that:
- Is designated as confidential, proprietary, or restricted by the Company; or

- Source code, algorithms, system architectures, and technical documentation
- Customer and prospect lists, contact data, and relationship intelligence
- Financial projections, pricing models, and unit economics

## 3. Non-Disclosure Obligations

The Recipient agrees to:
1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
2. Use Confidential Information only for the purpose of fulfilling their role with the Company
3. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent from the CLO
4. Restrict access to Confidential Information to those within the Company who have a need to know
5. Notify the Company immediately upon discovering any unauthorized disclosure or suspected breach

---

## 4. Exceptions

The non-disclosure obligation does not apply to information that the Recipient can demonstrate:
- Was publicly available at the time of disclosure through no fault of the Recipient
- Was independently developed by the Recipient without use of Confidential Information
- Was lawfully received from a third party without restriction
- Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, court order, or regulatory authority — provided the Company is given prior written notice and a reasonable opportunity to seek a protective order

---

## 5. Return of Materials

Upon termination of the engagement — or at the Company's written request — the Recipient must:
1. Return all Confidential Information in their possession or control
2. Permanently delete all copies from personal devices, cloud accounts, and email systems
3. Confirm compliance in writing within `<NEEDS_DECISION: number of days>`
