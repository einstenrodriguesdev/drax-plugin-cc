# JOB DESCRIPTIONS

> **Owner:** chro + each C-level
> **Approver:** ceo
> **Chain role:** Formalizes each role's scope and restrictions in writing (e.g. the CTO's function is managerial/strategic, with code micro-management and cross-area interference forbidden). Basis for CEO-run performance reviews.
> **Status:** DRAFT
> **Gate:** G-people (must be completed and approved before any performance review cycle opens)
> **Consistency:** must not contradict CHAIRMAN_LETTER.md or VISION.md.

---

## Purpose

This document contains the formal job description for each C-level role and, in summary form, for each Lead IC category in the organization. Each description defines the role's scope, key responsibilities, explicit restrictions, success metrics, and performance review basis. Formal job descriptions prevent scope creep, cross-area interference, and ambiguous accountability.

---

## How to Use This Template

Each role section below follows the same structure. Complete one section per role. The CHRO owns the drafting process; each C-level contributes their own section draft; the CEO approves all.

## Interview inputs (ask one at a time)

1. Which C-level roles exist in the company right now, and which are planned within the next 12 months?
2. For the CEO role: what is the single primary accountability in one sentence?
3. What restrictions must the CTO observe to prevent micro-management or cross-area interference?
4. For each C-level, which two or three metrics define success in the next 12 months?
5. How often will performance reviews occur — quarterly, biannual, annual?
6. Who conducts each C-level's performance review?
7. What cross-area interference scenarios has the founder already experienced that must be explicitly prohibited in writing?
8. What Lead IC categories exist, and do they need formal job descriptions at this stage?
9. For each role, are there responsibilities tied to the current growth stage that will evolve and should be flagged as temporary?
10. What inputs feed into a performance review — self-assessment, 360, metrics report, or some combination?
