## Purpose

The Articles of Incorporation is the foundational legal instrument that brings the company into legal existence. It establishes the company's name, registered address, corporate object, share structure, liability regime, and names the initial administrator. No external contract, employment agreement, or financing instrument has legal standing without this document being properly registered.

## Interview inputs (ask one at a time)

1. What is the full legal name of the company exactly as it will appear on registration documents?
2. What is the company's registered legal address?
3. In which country (and state/province, if relevant) is the company being incorporated?
4. Describe the company's legal object — what range of activities should it be authorized to engage in?
5. What is the total authorized share capital at incorporation, and in which currency?
6. How many shares will be authorized in total, and at what par value per share?
7. Who are the founding shareholders and what percentage does each hold?
8. Will there be more than one class of shares (e.g., ordinary and preferred)?
9. What liability regime applies — limited liability or another structure?
10. Who is the named CEO / legal administrator at incorporation?
11. Are there any restrictions on the CEO's signing authority — e.g., a cap on contract value they can sign alone?
12. What are the start and end dates of the company's fiscal year?
13. What voting threshold is required to amend these Articles?
