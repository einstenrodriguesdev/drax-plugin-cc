## Purpose

The Chairman Letter is the founding mandate of the company, written by and for the founder in the capacity of Chairman of the Board. It establishes the company's reason for existence, the non-negotiable principles that govern all decisions, and the standing instructions that the CEO must follow in daily execution. Every governance document in this structure derives its legitimacy from this letter.

## Interview inputs (ask one at a time)

1. In one or two sentences, what is this company's core mandate — the single problem it exists to solve and for whom?
2. What does "mission accomplished" look like in three years?
3. What is the primary domain of the company's activity (e.g., technology, education, marketing services)?
4. Are there activities the company is explicitly prohibited from pursuing?
5. What is your long-term vision — the ideal future state this company is building toward?
6. Name your top three non-negotiables: principles that must never be violated regardless of external pressure.
7. Why do you want a formal board and C-suite structure? What governance gap does it fill?
8. What is your personal relationship with the Board — Chair, member, or observer?
9. Which decisions must always return to you personally, even if everything else is delegated?
10. Are there geographic, ethical, or market lines the company will never cross?
11. What is your highest-level stance on data and customer privacy?
12. What are your standing behavioral instructions to the CEO — communication cadence, reporting obligations, escalation triggers?
13. Under what circumstances are you willing to intervene directly in CEO operations?
